About MexIHC 2020

MexIHC 2020 is the eighth Mexican Conference on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), the premier forum for researchers and practitioners to present advances in this field. The Mexican ACM SIGCHI chapter (CHI-México) and the Mexican Association on Human-Computer Interaction (AMexIHC) are the proud organizers of this biennial conference in which researchers, practitioners, faculty, students, institutions, and interest groups gather to network, discuss ideas, outline collaborative projects, and contribute to the consolidation and visibility of HCI in Mexico and Latin America.

MexIHC 2020 conference will take place in Morelia, Michoacán, a colonial city in central Mexico, which is one of the most beautiful and important tourist destinations in Mexico. The host for the conference will be the Universidad Michoacana de San Nicolás de Hidalgo, the first autonomous university in Mexico. Morelia is known for its invaluable cultural and historical heritage, Morelia is considered the cradle of the Independence Movement; and is home to several well-regarded cultural events like the Morelia Film Festival and all the festivities centered around the Dia de Muertos. Getting to Morelia is quite easy with plentiful of airline connections to all the major cities in Mexico and the U.S.

The theme of MexIHC 2020 is ​Inclusion.​ Inclusion is the attitude, tendency or policy of integrating all people into society, with the objective that they can participate and contribute to it and benefit from this process. Inclusion seeks to ensure that all individuals or social groups, especially those who are in conditions of segregation or marginalization (e.g., poverty, illiteracy, disability, ethnic or religious segregation, among other things), can have the same possibilities and opportunities to perform as individuals. We seek to provoke a lively discussion and new understandings of how HCI connects with knowledge, methodologies, and approaches from other disciplines to design, evaluate, and study the use and implications of interactive technology in the everyday life of people in order to enhance Inclusion in our society. We also want to encourage researchers and practitioners to bring out design and evaluation methods derived from established inquiry domains and design practices in HCI.

We invite original contributions on the theme of ​Inclusion and relevant topics in Human-Computer Interaction and Interaction Design. Papers will be double blind peer-reviewed by the members of the MexIHC 2020 program committee. Submissions will be evaluated based on their originality, significance of the contribution to the field and theme, technical correctness, and presentation.
