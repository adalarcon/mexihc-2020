Graduate Colloquium

The Graduate Colloquium provides a forum in which graduate students in computer science and related disciplines may develop the skill of publicly presenting research work and interests.

About the Graduate Colloquium

We invite to both master and doctoral students enrolled in graduate programs related to human-computer interaction (HCI) to submit a work in progress paper to be considered in the Graduate Colloquium of the VIII Mexican Conference on Human-Computer Interaction (MexIHC 2020).

Objective

The MexIHC 2020 graduate colloquium provides an opportunity for graduate students to share their research results and ideas in an interdisciplinary workshop. This workshop is conducted under the constructive guidance of a panel of distinguished researchers. Presenting at a graduate colloquium will help students to receive guidance during their research process and to present their work before their dissertation defense. Moreover, student participants will have the opportunity to find out the work of their peers, who will eventually become their potential collaborators researching problems related to HCI in Mexico.

Preparing the Submission Materials to the Graduate Colloquium

Each submission must be accompanied with the advisor's support letter and the work in progress paper should include:

  • Introduction.
  • Related Work.
  • Research objective.
  • Methods.
  • Current results.
  • Potential conclusions.
  • Information or aspects where I may need help with?
  • References.

The abstract of the research proposal must be written in English, use the Extended Abstract Format of the conference for up to 4 pages, and the student presenting at the colloquium should be the first author. The abstract will be published in the conference proceedings as extended abstracts. Student participants should be prepared to give a 10-minute talk about their work during the colloquium. At the end of each talk, we will hold a group discussion about their work. Papers should be sent to dc.2020@mexihc.org in a PDF format

As with other submissions sent to MexIHC 2020, the Students Design Competition entries should not have been previously accepted for presentation or publication in any other event.

Selection Criteria

This call is open to all graduate students with a research topic approved by their institution and whose dissertation defense is not scheduled before getting the feedback of the colloquium, so the student could clearly benefit from such feedback before graduating.

Clarity and quality of the submitted paper will be the primary criteria for the selection of the works, regardless of the degree of advancement of the research.

Important Dates

  • July 31st, 2020 – Notification.
  • August 14th, 2020 – Camera-ready submission
